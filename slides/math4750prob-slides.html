Brief Review of Probability

Sets (Events)

Set operations:

\begin{align*} A \cup B, A \cap B, A^c, A - B = A \setminus B = A \cap B^c. \end{align*}

Axioms of Probability

A.1 \(0\le \mathbb{P}(A)\le 1\) for each event \(A\);

A.2 \(\mathbb{P}(S) = 1\) and \(\mathbb{P}(\varnothing) =0\);

A.3 If \(A_1, A_2, \dots\) is a sequence of (pairwise) disjoint events, then

\[ \mathbb{P}\left( \bigcup_i A_i \right) = \sum_i \mathbb{P}(A_i).\quad(\text{Countable additivity}) \]

Addition Rule

\begin{align*} \mathbb{P}(A \cup B) = \mathbb{P}(A) + \mathbb{P}(B) - \mathbb{P}(A\cap B). \end{align*}

Conditional Probability

\begin{align*} \mathbb{P}(A | B) = \frac{\mathbb{P}(A \cap B)}{\mathbb{P}(B)}, \end{align*}

if \(\mathbb{P}(B) \neq 0\).

Multiplication Rule

\begin{align*} \mathbb{P}(A \cap B) = \mathbb{P}(AB) & = \mathbb{P}(A | B) \mathbb{P}(B)\\ & = \mathbb{P}(B | A) \mathbb{P}(A). \end{align*}

Law of Total Probability

\begin{align*} \mathbb{P}(A) & = \sum_{i=1}^n \mathbb{P}(A \cap B_i)\\ & = \sum_{i=1}^n \mathbb{P}(A | B_i) \mathbb{P}(B_i), \end{align*}

where \(\{B_i\}_{i=1}^n\) is a partition of the sample space \(S\).

Bayes Theorem

\begin{align*} \mathbb{P}(B_i |A) & = \frac{\mathbb{P}(A\cap B_i)}{\mathbb{P}(A)}\\ & = \frac{\mathbb{P}(B_i)\mathbb{P}(A|B_i)}{\sum_{j=1}^n \mathbb{P}(B_j) \mathbb{P}(A |B_j)}. \end{align*}

Random Variables

A random variable \(X\) is a function maps from the sample space \(S\) to the real line.

Probability mass function (p.m.f.) of a discrete random variable:

\begin{align*} p_X(k) = \mathbb{P}(X = k): \sum_{k} p_X(k) = 1. \end{align*}

Probability density function (p.d.f.) of a continuous random variable:

\begin{align*} f_X(x): \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_X(x)\, dx = 1. \end{align*}

Cumulative Distribution Function (C.D.F.) of a random variable:

\begin{align*} F_X(t) = \mathbb{P}(X \le t). \end{align*}

Properties?

In particular, for a continuous random variable \(X\),

\begin{align*} F_X(t) &= \int_{-\infty}^t f_X(x)\, dx\\ F'_X(t) &= f_X(t) \end{align*}

Bivariate Distributions

Only review the continuous case for simplicity.

Joint p.d.f.: \(f_{X, Y}(x,y)\)

\begin{align*} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{X, Y}(x, y)\, dx dy = 1. \end{align*}

Marginal p.d.f.:

\begin{align*} f_X(x) & = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{X, Y}(x, y)\, dy.\\ f_Y(y) & = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{X, Y}(x, y)\, dx.\\ \end{align*}

Conditional p.d.f.:

\begin{align*} f_{X|Y}(x | y) &= \frac{f_{X, Y}(x, y)}{f_Y(y)}\\ f_{Y|X}(y | x) &= \frac{f_{X, Y}(x, y)}{f_X(x)}\\ \end{align*}

Expectation (weighted average)

\begin{align*} \mu = \mathbb{E}(X) = \begin{cases} \sum_k k p_X(k)\\ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x f_X(x)\, dx. \end{cases} \end{align*}

LOTUS:

\begin{align*} \mathbb{E}(g(X)) = \begin{cases} \sum_k g(k) p_X(k)\\ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} g(x) f_X(x)\, dx. \end{cases} \end{align*}

Properties of Expectation

  • \(\mathbb{E}(a X + b) = a \mathbb{E}(X) + b\).
  • \(\mathbb{E}(X + Y) = \mathbb{E}(X) + \mathbb{E}(Y)\).
  • \(\mathbb{E}(XY) = \mathbb{E}(X)\mathbb{E}(Y)\), if \(X \perp Y\).

Variance

\begin{align*} \text{Var} (X) &= \mathbb{E}(X - \mu)^2\\ & = \mathbb{E}(X^2) - [\mathbb{E}(X)]^2. \end{align*}

We call \(\sigma_X = \sqrt{\text{Var} (X)}\) the standard deviation of \(X\).

Moments

\(\mathbb{E}(X^k)\), for \(k = 0, 1, 2, \dots\)

Moment generating function (m.g.f.):

\begin{align*} \psi_X(t) = \mathbb{E}(e^{tX}). \end{align*}

Theorem: \(\mathbb{E}(X^k) = \psi_X^{(k)}(0)\).

Covariance

\begin{align*} \text{Cov} (X, Y) & = \mathbb{E}[(X - \mu_X)(Y - \mu_Y)]\\ & = \mathbb{E}(XY) - \mathbb{E}(X)\mathbb{E}(Y). \end{align*}

In particular,

\begin{align*} \text{Cov} (X, X) = \text{Var} (X). \end{align*}

Correlation

\begin{align*} \rho_{X,Y} = \frac{\text{Cov} (X, Y) }{\sigma_X \sigma_Y}, \quad -1\le \rho_{X, Y} \le 1. \end{align*}

Example: If \(X + Y = 5\), what is \(\rho_{X, Y}\)?

Answer: \(-1\).

Formulas

How to expand \(\text{Var}(X + Y)\) and \(\text{Cov}(X + Y, U + V)\)?

\begin{align*} \text{Var} (X + Y) = \text{Var} (X) + \text{Var} (Y) + 2 \text{Cov} (X, Y). \end{align*}
\begin{align*} \text{Cov} (X+Y, U+V) & = \text{Cov} (X, U) + \text{Cov} (X, V)\\ & \quad + \text{Cov} (Y, U) + \text{Cov} (Y, V). \end{align*}

Independence

We say two events \(A\) and \(B\) are independent if

\begin{align*} \mathbb{P}(AB) = \mathbb{P}(A) \mathbb{P}(B). \end{align*}

Questions: What are other equivalent definitions?

For random variables:

\begin{align*} X \perp Y \Leftrightarrow & f_{X, Y}(x, y) = f_X(x) f_Y(y)\\ & f_{X|Y}(x|y) = f_X(x)\\ \Rightarrow & \mathbb{E} (XY) = \mathbb{E}(X) \mathbb{E}(Y)\\ \Rightarrow & \text{Cov} (X, Y) = 0, \rho_{X, Y} = 0. \end{align*}
